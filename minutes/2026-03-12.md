     - Susannah - when we talk about future generations, it tends to relate to future availability of resources. So sustainability is in terms of not over consuming resources and thus harming that future availability.

     - James - questioning what happens if TAG takes exception to a particular part of a definition. A bounded definition should help to mitigate this possibility.

     - Alex - other areas of W3C and beyond have similar definition-type issues within ESG. Being able to link the way in which particular parts have cross dimensional aspects will help us underpin any definition we come up with. Environmental aspects are more visible in many people’s minds than Social and Governance.

     - Tzviya - TAG will not tell us we’re doing sustainability wrong, more that a process hasn’t been followed, working hasn’t been shown etc. Need to begin with referenced upfront definition as a foundation.

       - Tzviya - most of W3C docs are tech focused, how to build etc. and don’t address organisational aspects. WCAG maturity model exists as a note only - it’s widely used, but contentious.

       - Sustainability is different because a clear and crisp definition is required for the audience. No-one reads glossaries
